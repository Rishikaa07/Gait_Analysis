Introduction

Overview

Reverse walking (backward walking) is a complex motor task that challenges balance, proprioception, and motor coordination. It has gained interest in both clinical rehabilitation and gait research due to its potential to reveal hidden deficits in neuromuscular control and biomechanical adaptation.

Why It Matters

Understanding reverse walking provides insight into compensatory mechanisms under constrained gait conditions. It also serves as a diagnostic and rehabilitative tool for identifying early balance impairments, making it highly valuable in neurorehabilitation and athletic training.
