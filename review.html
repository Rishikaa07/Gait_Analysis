Review of Literature

Background

Previous studies on gait biomechanics highlight the importance of muscle activation patterns and kinematic changes in constrained gait. Reverse walking has been explored as a tool to challenge neuromuscular coordination and provide unique rehabilitation benefits.

Key Findings

  • Reverse walking enhances proprioceptive feedback and balance control.
  • Thermal imaging assists in mapping muscle activity during gait tasks.
  • VR-based gait analysis improves engagement in rehabilitation.
